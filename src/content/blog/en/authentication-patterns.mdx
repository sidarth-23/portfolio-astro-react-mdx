## The Problem with Simple JWT

Most tutorials show JWT as the silver bullet for authentication. But storing tokens client-side opens doors to XSS attacks, and long-lived tokens are a security nightmare when compromised.

## Sessions vs Tokens

**Sessions** (server-side):
- ✅ Revocable instantly
- ✅ Can store arbitrary data
- ❌ Requires server state
- ❌ harder to scale horizontally

**JWTs** (client-side):
- ✅ Stateless — scales easily
- ✅ Cross-service friendly
- ❌ Cannot revoke without blocklist
- ❌ Payload visible to client

## The Hybrid Approach

Use short-lived access tokens (JWT) + long-lived refresh tokens (session-based). This gives you the best of both worlds: scalability and revocability.

## Implementation Tips

1. Keep access tokens short (5-15 minutes)
2. Store refresh tokens in httpOnly cookies
3. Rotate refresh tokens on every use
4. Maintain a token blocklist for logout

## Bottom Line

There's no one-size-fits-all. Choose based on your scale, security requirements, and team expertise.
